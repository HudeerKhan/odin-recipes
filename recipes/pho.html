Pho

Description

Pho is a traditional Vietnamese noodle soup known for its aromatic and flavorful broth, typically made from simmered beef or chicken bones. It features rice noodles (bánh phở), and is often served with a variety of fresh herbs, bean sprouts, lime wedges, and chili peppers, allowing each person to customize their bowl to taste. The rich broth, combined with tender meat and vibrant garnishes, makes pho a comforting and satisfying dish.

Check out Quang Tran's pho recipe on youtube as a variation to this one.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef bones or chicken bones
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 1 piece of ginger, about 2 inches, sliced
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
  • 8 oz rice noodles
  • 1/2 lb thinly sliced beef (such as sirloin) or cooked chicken
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Bean sprouts
  • Lime wedges
  • Thai basil leaves
  • Sliced jalapeños or chili peppers (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Broth:

    2. In a large pot, add the beef or chicken bones and cover with water. Bring to a boil and then reduce the heat, simmering for about 10 minutes. Discard the water and rinse the bones to remove impurities.

  2. Simmer the Broth:

    3. In the same pot, add the cleaned bones, onion, ginger, star anise, and cinnamon stick. Pour in 4 cups of fresh water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for at least 1-2 hours, skimming off any foam that rises to the surface.

  3. Season the Broth:

    4. After simmering, strain the broth to remove the solids. Return the clear broth to the pot and season with fish sauce, salt, and sugar (if using). Adjust seasoning to taste.

  4. Cook the Noodles:

    5. Prepare the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  5. Assemble the Pho:

    6. Divide the cooked rice noodles among bowls. Top with thinly sliced beef or cooked chicken. Pour the hot broth over the noodles and meat.

  6. Add Toppings:

    7. Garnish with fresh cilantro, bean sprouts, lime wedges, and Thai basil leaves. Add sliced jalapeños or chili peppers if desired.

  7. Serve:

    8. Serve the pho hot, allowing each person to add more garnishes or sauces according to their taste.
